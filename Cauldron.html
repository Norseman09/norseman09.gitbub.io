Cauldron


An all-in-one app for Dungeons & Dragons


Creator: Andrew Waddell, Artificial Intelligence


Last Updated: 09/27/2023
Technical Field:
My project encompasses the technical fields of computer programming, A.I., digital art, and mobile app development.
Background Information:

I am pursuing this project for a few different reasons. First is that I want to be able help newer players understand the game, create characters with ease, and be able to have everything they need right in front of them. Secondly, I want to give both new and veteran players as well as dungeon masters an all-encompassing app for their use rather than having to constantly manage multiple apps for their games. Lastly, I want to be able to give the dungeon masters an all-encompassing app that allows them to manage combat, encounters, magical items, and the like. This will take some weight off their shoulders and allow them to better manage the game they are running.

Prior Art (legal term):

While there are a hand full of other apps that do one or two things such as encounter management or item generation the only two real competitors are D&D Beyond and PrismScroll's app Prism. Both have decent functionality with the ability to manage campaigns for Dungeon Masters, create characters and view item/spell lists. However, many of the features are hidden behind a paywall, meaning that you must buy additional access or purchase a subscription in order to utilize all the features. Whereas my app will be a onetime fee for access to everything a player or DM would need. The user friendliness of Prism is mediocre at best. Along with the off-putting color scheme the app is rather confusing for first time users and is one of the main reasons I found that would turn others away from it. D&D Beyond is the official app of Dungeons & Dragons. However, I also found the app a little confusing at first. Many of the features of this app are hidden behind paywalls as well. They do provide basic rules for character creation and game mechanics, however anything beyond that requires purchase of specific books or subscriptions. Neither of these apps utilize A.I. in any way. My app will leverage A.I. to assist in character creation, background story, narration, settings, NPC generation, and many other things to make it easier for players and DMs alike to enjoy the game.

Project Description:

This project will create an all-in-one style app that will be easy to use for both new and veteran players and DMs. It will help new players learn the basics of the game quickly, create characters with ease and on the fly while helping veteran players play out their perfect character or campaign. It will assist DMs in managing the campaign and all the aspects such as loot, encounters, story arch, etc.

Claim:

This app will create an all-in-one source for both new and veteran players and dungeon masters. It will leverage the use of current A.I. technology that will assist in character creation, item creation, background stories, story arch, narration, NPC generation, and help bring in new players while also assisting veteran ones hone their skills as players/DMs. This will allow everyone to create new ways to enjoy the game.

Usage Scenario:

The target demographic for this application would be any person who is new to the game Dungeons & Dragons. This application is designed for the players who have never played the game but are wanting to get into it. Due to the style of this app, veteran players will also benefit from the usage of an all-in-one app for all things D&D, to include simplified rules, lists of class and subclass, spells, weapons and gear, etc. The app will give new players the confidence they need to join a campaign of veteran players or create their own party to play the game. The app will publish on both iOS and Android to reach both sides of the smart phone isle with a web-based browser in the future. This will easily target the over 50 million players of D&D.

    • Description of Design Prototype:

    After researching and interacting with my competition's apps, my main goal for design is user friendliness. The design layout of the D&D Beyond app is substantially better than that of the Prism app. My design will mimic some of that user friendliness so new players can easily navigate the app to where they want to go. The app will run on both iOS and Android; however, I am going to first create the app for iOS. The reason being is that I will be able to reach a broader target market in the first iteration of app creation. It seems that more people, in my experience, possess an iPhone over an Android phone. Upon clicking or tapping the app icon, which will be a Cauldron, the user will be greeted with a selection menu. They will be able to choose player or DM and from there they will have access to either a player's area or DM area. These areas will have all the open-source information, simplified wherever possible, rules, character sheets, etc. based on if they are a player or DM. The layout will have different section icons on the bottom with a search bar on top. Players/DMs will be able to easily navigate through the app and find the items/rules/creatures/etc. they are looking for. The font and text will lean more toward Viking style writing over the original D&D font style.
